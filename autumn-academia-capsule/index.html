The Capsule Edit · Dress Like a Poet

An autumn academia wardrobe from a few good pieces.

Six vintage-leaning staples that layer into a full dark academia autumn — no designer budget, no overflowing wardrobe.

The dark academia look isn't about owning a lot. It's about a few considered, moody pieces — tweed, wool, leather and ink — that all talk to each other and layer for the cold.

Build this small capsule once and you'll dress for the whole of autumn from it. Everything here is simple to find now; swap in vintage and thrifted versions as you hunt them down.

First, the capsule rules

How to build the capsule

Five rules for a wardrobe that looks curated, not crowded.

  1. 1Start with a blazer. A tweed or wool blazer is the backbone — everything layers under or over it.
  2. 2Add texture, not colour. Stay in a moody palette — oxblood, forest, camel, ink, cream — and mix textures instead.
  3. 3One bag, one boot. A leather satchel and a pair of leather boots carry every outfit — buy well once.
  4. 4Layer knits. An oversized turtleneck and a chunky cardigan turn two outfits into six.
  5. 5Keep it thrift-first. Buy the timeless shapes; hunt the vintage versions over time to keep it cheap and unique.
