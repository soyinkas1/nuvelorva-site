A dark academia wardrobe on a budget.

You don't need designer labels for the poetcore look — just the right shapes in the right moody tones. Here's the affordable, thrift-first edit.

The academia look is about shape and tone, not price — a boxy blazer, a soft knit, a pleated skirt in the right colours reads as considered whatever it cost.

Buy these affordable staples now, and swap in charity-shop finds as you hunt them down. Here's the budget edit.

First, the budget rules

How to do it cheaply

Five rules for the look on a small budget.

  1. 1Shape over label. Look for the right cut — boxy blazer, high-waist skirt — not the brand.
  2. 2Stay in the palette. Brown, oxblood, cream, charcoal, ink. Colour discipline reads as expensive.
  3. 3Thrift the blazers. Blazers and coats are the best charity-shop value — hunt there first.
  4. 4Buy new only for basics. Plain knits, shirts and tights are worth buying new and cheap.
  5. 5Accessorise last. One leather-look bag and boots pull budget pieces together.
